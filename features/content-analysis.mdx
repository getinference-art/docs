The Content Analysis card measures your **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** performance - how effectively your property or brand appears when AI models like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity generate answers to traveler queries. Every metric is grounded in peer-reviewed academic research on what makes content more visible to AI.

## Key Metrics

### Position-Adjusted Word Count

This is your single most important metric for understanding overall AI visibility volume. It measures the total number of words about your brand across all AI responses, weighted by where those mentions appear.

Earlier mentions count more than later ones because AI responses are read top-to-bottom. Being mentioned first means travelers see you before competitors.

**How to read it:** A higher number means more prominent, better-positioned visibility across AI answers.

<Tip>
Think of this as a combined measure of how often and how prominently AI features your brand. It captures both volume and quality of placement in a single number.
</Tip>

### Relevance Score (0-100)

How strongly AI models associate your brand with the search queries being tracked. A high relevance score means AI considers you a strong match for what travelers are asking.

This metric combines two factors: how frequently your brand appears in responses, and how early in the response your first mention occurs.

| Range | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 80-100 | Excellent | AI strongly associates you with these queries |
| 60-79 | Good | Solid presence, room for optimization |
| 40-59 | Fair | Inconsistent appearance in AI answers |
| 0-39 | Needs Improvement | AI rarely considers you relevant to these queries |

<Note>
A low relevance score means your online content is not signaling to AI that you are a relevant answer. This is the "are we even showing up?" metric.
</Note>

### Influence Score (0-100)

When your brand IS mentioned, how much space and prominence does it get? A brand can be mentioned but barely visible (low influence) or can dominate the response (high influence).

This metric combines your share of the response length with the presence of credibility signals like statistics, quotes, and citations near your mentions.

**How to read it:** Even if you are mentioned, a tiny reference among 2,000 words has minimal impact on a traveler's decision. This tells you whether AI is giving you meaningful coverage or just a passing mention.

### Trend Indicator

The arrow next to your Position-Adjusted Word Count compares your recent performance to your earlier performance within the tracking period:

- **Up arrow (green)** - Recent responses show more visibility than older ones
- **Down arrow (red)** - Recent responses show less visibility
- **Flat line (gray)** - No significant change

## Content Quality Signals

These three indicators are based on published GEO research showing that specific content types dramatically boost AI visibility. The impact percentages come from the original research.

### Statistics (+41.2% visibility improvement)

The percentage of AI responses mentioning your brand that also include numbers, data, or statistics nearby.

**What counts:** Percentages, room counts, guest numbers, square footage, prices, star ratings, and numerical ratings.

**Why it matters:** Numbers and data points are the single highest-impact content signal. AI models prefer citing sources that provide concrete, verifiable data.

<Tip>
Compare these two descriptions: "spacious rooms" versus "42 luxury suites averaging 55 square meters." The second version is dramatically more likely to be cited by AI.
</Tip>

### Quotations (+38.5% visibility improvement)

The percentage of AI responses mentioning your brand that also include direct quotes or testimonials nearby.

**What counts:** Text in quotation marks, or attribution phrases like "according to," "said," "stated," or "reported."

**Why it matters:** Direct quotes add authenticity and trustworthiness. Guest reviews, press quotes, and expert testimonials give AI models concrete, attributable content to reference.

### Technical Terms (+18.6% visibility improvement)

The average number of industry-specific terms found near your brand mentions per AI response.

**What counts:** Terms like "concierge," "all-inclusive," "infinity pool," "butler service," "Michelin," "eco-certified," and other hospitality vocabulary.

**Why it matters:** Industry-specific terminology signals authority and expertise. AI models trained on high-quality content associate technical language with authoritative sources.

## How to Improve Your Scores

<Steps>

### Add statistics and data points to your website

This has the highest proven impact at +41.2%. Include room counts, guest capacity, distances to landmarks, star ratings, review scores, award counts, prices, package values, and square footage.

### Add testimonials and quotes

At +38.5% impact, this is the second most effective lever. Feature guest reviews with direct quotes, press mentions, expert endorsements, and award citations with quoted criteria.

### Add credible citations and sources

Reference authoritative tourism boards, industry certifications, sustainability credentials, and third-party ratings like TripAdvisor or Google reviews with proper attribution.

### Use industry terminology

Replace generic language with hospitality terms. Instead of "nice rooms," write "luxury suites with turndown service." Instead of "good food," write "Michelin-recommended dining with half-board options."

### Optimize content structure for AI

Create FAQ pages that directly answer common travel questions. Use structured data on your website. Ensure your Google Business Profile is complete and accurate. Build authoritative backlinks from tourism boards and travel publications.

</Steps>

## How Quickly Will Scores Improve?

These metrics update as AI models pick up your enriched content. Typical timelines:

- **Google Business Profile updates** reflect in days to weeks
- **Structured data improvements** can show impact within 1-2 weeks for some AI models
- **Website content changes** take 2-8 weeks to be indexed by AI training data
- **Consistent improvement compounds** over time, as each signal reinforces the others

<Note>
There is no instant fix. AI visibility is built through sustained content quality improvements. The good news is that every improvement you make reinforces the others, creating a compounding effect over time.
</Note>
